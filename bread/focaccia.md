# ned's lazy focaccia (2 hrs lead time)

- 850g warm water
- 10-20g (1-2 Tbsp) instant yeast
- 750g bread flour
- 30g olive oil
- 1 Tbsp sale

Instructions:
- Bloom the yeast for 30 minutes or until bubbling.
- Combine all ingredients. Protect hands with oil while stretch n folding.
- Let dough rise until swollen, 30-60 min. Knead it again; at this point it should hold its shape a little. If it doesn't, add more flour.
- Cajole the dough onto a 12"x18" tray, oiled or lined with a Silpat. Let rise 20 min.
- Preheat oven 500F, or 550F if possible.
- Sprinkle tons of rosemary on top; you can't have too much. Then sprinkle salt, the larger the better.
- Use your fingers to squish wells into the dough. Pour a tablespoon or so of olive oil on top, getting some in the wells.
- Start baking. After 8 minutes check; if it’s browning on the top, turn temp to 450 F then, otherwise wait 2 minutes before doing so.
- When it’s been in for ten minutes at 450 F, take the tray out.
- To prevent the bottom from sogging, put the loaf on a cooling rack or other position where the bottom can steam freely.
- Hardest part of the recipe: wait 15m before eating.
